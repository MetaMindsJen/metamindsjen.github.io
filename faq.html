Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Why does my AI make up info it doesn't know?

A: The AI may occasionally generate information it doesn't know because it's designed to provide a response based on the context and patterns in the data it was trained on. Some commands have a form of long-term memory through log searches. Usually those searches look for your whole command (after the !command) and if they don’t find a specific match, they start looking for any word in your command. This can sometimes lead to plausible-sounding but incorrect answers.

Q: If I give him the right answer, will he remember?

A: The AI will retain the information in the context thread and short-term memory, but not long-term memory. We're working on improving this aspect. Currently, you can use !truth and !lies commands to establish facts. Generally, !truth works better. We also pre-load our AIs with information about their respective communities.

Q: If you add him to another server, do you configure him specifically for that server? Or is it the same bot with the information from the previous server?

A: Absolutely, we fully customize the AI for each server. It will have a new personality based on your community and a new mission aligned with your project, which it will consistently refer back to.

Q: What's the best way to reach out to you?

A: You can reach us via Discord.

Q: Do you have a basic guide on how to give AI prompts?

Here's a step-by-step guide for composing the perfect prompt, even for someone who is new to AI or speaks English as a second language:

  • Be clear and concise: Start by clearly stating your question or request. Keep your sentences short and to the point, so the AI can easily understand what you're asking.
  • Provide context: Give some background information or context to help the AI understand the topic better. This can include relevant details, examples, or explanations.
  • Specify the format: If you have a preferred format for the AI's response, make it explicit. For instance, you may want the answer in a list, a paragraph, or a step-by-step guide.
  • Ask for examples or explanations: If you'd like the AI to provide examples or further explanations in its response, make sure to request them in your prompt.
  • Keep it focused: Avoid asking multiple questions or covering too many topics in a single prompt. This can confuse the AI and result in less accurate or less relevant responses.

Here's an example prompt following these guidelines:


"Can you please explain the process of photosynthesis in plants, step by step? I'd like a simple explanation with an example for each step, suitable for someone with no background in biology."


By following these steps, you'll be able to craft effective prompts that help the AI provide the information or assistance you need.
